# Additional improvements incorporated v1.7.2

Beyond simply responding to the red-team review, this release adds several hardening improvements:

1. **Counter-hypothesis made explicit.** The current reform pathway may be enough; this is now stated as the strongest alternative interpretation.
2. **Weak-control scenario rejected in plain language.** Uncapped activity without rules is treated as a failure mode, not a policy option.
3. **Rural caveat strengthened.** Fee-for-service alone will not solve rurality; workforce, housing, capital, infrastructure and salaried models may be needed.
4. **Scope-fragmentation risk elevated.** Scope-enabled supply requires shared records, continuity and governance.
5. **Hospital-causality humility added.** Upstream access is a plausible contributor to hospital pressure, not a single-cause explanation.
6. **RACMA removed from launch pathway.** Engagement is public-first and validation-first; RACMA can re-enter only if interested.
7. **Launch posts now carry red-team checks.** Each first-six post explicitly addresses the most relevant critique.
