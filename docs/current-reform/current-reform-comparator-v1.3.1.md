# Current reform comparator and uncapped FFS alternative — v1.3.1

The comparator remains the current reform pathway: capitation reweighting, primary care access target, National Primary Care Dataset, urgent/after-hours expansion, digital access, PHO accountability/future-function work and workforce initiatives.

The proposed alternative is not merely current reform plus a larger capped pool. It is:

> **Current reform + uncapped primary medical FFS entitlement + capitation + place-based accountability.**

## Scenario logic

1. **Legacy baseline** — capitation/fixed-envelope logic dominates.
2. **Current reform pathway** — improves weights, targets, data and urgent care, but remains largely within managed funding architecture.
3. **Current reform + place accountability** — strengthens anti-cherry-picking and equity obligations.
4. **Uncapped medical FFS without place accountability** — restores supply signal but risks easy-work growth and fiscal leakage.
5. **Uncapped medical FFS with capitation and place accountability** — preferred architecture.
6. **Uncapped weak-control model** — deliberately bad comparator.
7. **ACC-style medical-only stream** — tests whether medical FFS without broader team/scope/place reform is enough.
8. **Full hybrid upstream architecture** — combines uncapped medical FFS, scope-enabled supply, urgent/ambulance alternatives, capitation, place accountability and data/audit.

## Key conclusion

The cap should be removed at the **global funding-envelope level**, while governance should be strengthened at the **contact, provider, patient and place level**.
