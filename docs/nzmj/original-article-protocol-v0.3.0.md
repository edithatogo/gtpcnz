# Protocol: Primary care funding as a repeated game

## Submission strategy

Use the Viewpoint first if the paper remains conceptual. Submit as an Original Article only if empirical simulation outputs, validated game-theoretic results or reproducible policy-mapping outputs are available.

## Working title

**Primary care funding as a repeated game: a policy and simulation-modelling framework for Aotearoa New Zealand and Australia**

## Aims

1. Map funding and accountability architecture for primary, urgent, ambulance/prehospital and hospital care in Aotearoa New Zealand, with Australia as a comparator.
2. Develop a repeated-game model of upstream funding constraint and downstream hospital growth.
3. Specify system dynamics and agent-based simulation models for alternative funding architectures.
4. Identify empirically testable scenarios.

## Methods

### Policy architecture mapping

Map actors, funding streams, administrative intermediaries, provider eligibility, co-payment settings, performance targets and data flows.

### Game-theoretic modelling

Define players, strategies, payoff variables and repeated-game dynamics. Test conditions under which hospital-rescue equilibrium dominates upstream investment.

### System dynamics modelling

Represent feedback loops between primary care supply, unmet need, ambulance/ED demand, hospital pressure, provider viability and budget response.

### Agent-based modelling

Represent heterogeneous patients, providers and funders. Model patient choice, provider entry/exit, co-payment sensitivity, scope-based supply and rural access.

## Scenarios

1. Status quo.
2. Capitation reweighting only.
3. Capitation reweighting plus access target.
4. Reweighting plus limited activity-sensitive payments.
5. National Primary Care Benefits Schedule.
6. Benefits Schedule plus broad provider eligibility.
7. Benefits Schedule plus rural/ambulance contact types.
8. Reduced PHO intermediation.
9. Constrained ACC fee-for-service.
10. Australia shared-savings/Commonwealth-state alignment.

## Outcomes

- timely primary care access;
- same-day urgent access;
- unmet need;
- provider supply;
- rural in-person capacity;
- co-payment burden;
- ED demand;
- ambulance conveyance and non-conveyance;
- avoidable hospitalisation;
- total public cost;
- equity by ethnicity, deprivation, disability, rurality, age and multimorbidity.

## Reporting standards

- PRISMA-ScR or PRISMA 2020 for review components.
- ODD protocol for agent-based modelling.
- STRESS for empirical simulation studies.
- CHEERS 2022 if economic evaluation is added.

## Ethics

Public-data conceptual modelling may not require HREC approval. Stakeholder interviews, identifiable data or unpublished administrative data require ethics and data-governance review.

## Article decision rule

Submit as Article only if results are populated with validated empirical outputs. Otherwise, submit the Viewpoint.
