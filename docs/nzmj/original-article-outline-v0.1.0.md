# NZMJ original article outline

## Working title

**Primary care funding as a repeated game: a policy and simulation-modelling framework for Aotearoa New Zealand and Australia**

## Target journal

New Zealand Medical Journal.

## Submission type

Preferred: **Article** if simulation outputs are available.  
Fallback: **Viewpoint** if the paper remains conceptual and policy-analytic.

NZMJ article guidance requires a broad readership style, a structured abstract not exceeding 200 words using Aims, Methods, Results and Conclusions, a brief introduction followed by Methods, Results and Discussion, a 3000-word limit and 30-reference limit. Viewpoints also have a 3000-word and 30-reference limit.

## Research question

Can game-theoretic mapping and simulation modelling clarify how primary care, ambulance and hospital funding rules may channel system growth into hospitals, and identify alternative funding architectures for New Zealand and Australia?

## Article structure

### Abstract

Aims, Methods, Results, Conclusions.

### Introduction

- Primary care reform is usually framed around funding quantum.
- This paper reframes it as architecture and repeated-game equilibrium.
- NZ and Australia offer contrasting but linked cases.

### Methods

1. Policy architecture mapping.
2. Game-theoretic actor/strategy/payoff mapping.
3. Causal loop diagram.
4. Preliminary system dynamics or agent-based model specification.
5. Scenario definition.

### Results

Potential result categories:

- Actor map.
- Repeated-game structure.
- Hypothesised equilibria.
- Scenario logic.
- Model outputs once available.

### Discussion

- Capitation reweighting may improve allocation but not remove supply constraints.
- Demand-driven contact benefits may shift equilibrium.
- PHO function/intermediation distinction.
- ACC and ambulance spillover effects.
- Equity and co-payment risk.
- Implications for top-tier KPIs.

## Figures

1. Funding architecture map.
2. Repeated game/payoff matrix.
3. Causal loop diagram.
4. Scenario comparison table.

## Preferred submission pathway

1. Finish conceptual model and evidence review.
2. Build minimal simulation prototype.
3. Produce initial model outputs.
4. Submit as Article.

## Fallback pathway

Submit as Viewpoint:
