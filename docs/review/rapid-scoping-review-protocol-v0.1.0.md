# Rapid scoping review protocol

## Title

Primary care funding architecture and its effects on supply, access and hospital demand: a rapid scoping review.

## Aim

To identify and summarise evidence on the effects of fee-for-service, capitation, blended funding, programme funding and direct payment architectures on primary care supply, access, equity, provider behaviour, market entry and hospital demand.

## Review questions

1. What is known about the effect of capitation on service supply, under-provision, waiting times, enrolment and provider behaviour?
2. What is known about the effect of fee-for-service on marginal supply, volume, cost and low-value care?
3. What blended payment models are used internationally and what outcomes have been reported?
4. What is known about transaction costs and administrative intermediaries in primary care funding?
5. What evidence links primary care investment or access to ED presentations, ambulatory-sensitive hospitalisations or hospital admissions?
6. What evidence exists for multidisciplinary provider generation of primary care activity?
7. What evidence exists for ambulance treat-and-refer/hear-and-treat pathways as hospital-avoidance mechanisms?

## Inclusion criteria

- Primary care, urgent care, community health or ambulance/prehospital care.
- Payment/funding/incentive architecture.
- High-income country settings, with priority to NZ, Australia, UK, Canada, Netherlands, US integrated models and OECD comparators.
- Empirical studies, reviews, policy evaluations, simulation models and high-quality grey literature.

## Exclusion criteria

- Purely hospital funding studies unless directly linked to primary care incentives.
- Disease-specific payment models without generalisable funding mechanism.
- Opinion pieces without policy or theoretical contribution, unless from major official reviews.

## Databases

MEDLINE, Embase, CINAHL, Scopus, Web of Science, EconLit, Cochrane, OECD, WHO, Commonwealth Fund, Australian Department of Health, AIHW, NZ Ministry of Health, Health NZ, Treasury, ACC, Sapere and relevant college submissions.

## Data extraction

Use `extraction-matrix-template-v0.1.0.csv`.

## Synthesis

Narrative synthesis by mechanism and outcome. Evidence certainty labelled as high/moderate/low/unclear.

## Reporting

Use PRISMA-ScR where appropriate.
