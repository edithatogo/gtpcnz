# Eligibility criteria for rapid scoping review v0.5.0

## Population / system

Primary care, general practice, urgent primary care, community pharmacy, nurse practitioner-led care, allied health first-contact care, prehospital/ambulance alternative disposition, and hospital-avoidance interfaces.

## Concepts

Include studies or policy analyses addressing at least one of:

- fee-for-service/activity-sensitive payment;
- capitation or enrolled-population payment;
- blended payment models;
- pay-for-performance and target-linked payments;
- direct claims platforms or benefit schedules;
- provider-scope expansion and multidisciplinary primary care;
- PHO/PHN/intermediary commissioning or transaction cost;
- co-payment and affordability effects;
- primary care access and ED/hospital/ambulance substitution;
- rural primary care supply and telehealth substitution/complementarity.

## Context

Prioritise New Zealand and Australia, then high-income comparable health systems.

## Study/source types

Include:

- systematic reviews and scoping reviews;
- economic evaluations and simulation studies;
- quasi-experimental and observational studies;
- experimental economics/lab studies where provider payment incentives are directly relevant;
- official reviews, ministerial briefings and policy reports;
- credible sector submissions where no stronger source exists.

Exclude:

- opinion pieces without evidence or mechanism;
- hospital-only funding studies not linked to primary/community/ambulance interface;
- specialist outpatient-only payment studies unless relevant to primary care substitution;
- non-health payment literature unless used for theory only.

## Timeframe

- Payment-model evidence: 2000 onward, with older theory allowed where foundational.
- Policy architecture: 2010 onward, with older NZ capitation documents included as necessary.
- Current policy sources: latest available version.

## Languages

English.
