# Screening codebook v0.5.0

## Include if the source answers at least one question

1. Does a payment model affect primary care supply, access, utilisation, quality, equity, cost or hospital demand?
2. Does a funding architecture affect market entry, provider behaviour, transaction cost or accountability?
3. Does a policy document describe NZ or Australian primary care, ambulance or hospital funding architecture?
4. Does a simulation or economic model estimate effects of changing provider payment?
5. Does evidence address co-payment, scope expansion, telehealth substitution or ambulance alternative disposition?

## Exclusion codes

- X1: Not primary care/community/ambulance/hospital interface.
- X2: No payment, funding, incentive, access or supply relevance.
- X3: Not a credible source type.
- X4: Duplicate.
- X5: Full text unavailable after reasonable search.
- X6: Wrong country/context and no transferable mechanism.
- X7: Pure commentary with no evidence or analytic mechanism.

## Mechanism codes

- M1: Marginal supply incentive.
- M2: Under-provision under fixed payment.
- M3: Over-provision/low-value activity under activity payment.
- M4: Transaction cost/intermediation.
- M5: Principal-agent/cross-funder externality.
- M6: Hospital avoidance or substitution.
- M7: Equity/affordability.
- M8: Professional scope/supply elasticity.
- M9: Telehealth substitution/complementarity.
- M10: KPI salience/target gaming.
- M11: Ambulance alternative disposition.

## Evidence strength coding

- A: Direct empirical evidence from NZ/Australia.
- B: Direct empirical evidence from comparable health systems.
- C: Systematic/scoping review evidence.
- D: Official policy/administrative evidence.
- E: Simulation/model evidence.
- F: Theory only.
