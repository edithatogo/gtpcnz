# Model validation v1.7.2

This page states the current evidence position plainly. The model is a public-data anchored benchmark. It is useful for testing the architecture logic and prioritising validation work, but it is not a linked-data calibrated or patient-level forecasting model.

## Evidence status

- The benchmark is complete enough to compare funding architectures and identify load-bearing assumptions.
- It is not empirically calibrated to linked New Zealand administrative data.
- Its outputs should be read as structured hypotheses, not as precise effect estimates.

## Missing calibration data

- Linked primary care appointment, encounter, open-book, co-payment and workforce data.
- Linked primary care, ambulance, emergency department and inpatient flow data.
- Provider revenue mix, PHO pass-through and intermediation data.
- Equity-stratified access, continuity and outcome data.
- Before-and-after reform data strong enough to test whether the current pathway already solves the access problem.

## OIA dependencies

- Health New Zealand National Primary Care Dataset metadata and aggregate access reporting.
- PHO financial pass-through, contract and administrative friction material.
- ACC claims, contracts and activity-payment evidence.
- Ambulance disposition, emergency department and inpatient linkage.
- Governance and privacy material needed to join the above datasets safely.

## Falsification tests

- Reweighted capitation plus current access targets materially increase useful appointment supply without a new activity-sensitive payment stream.
- Access constraints do not predict emergency department, ambulance or admission flow after adjusting for need, rurality and deprivation.
- ACC activity payments do not materially support provider viability, activity or local access.
- PHO intermediation is transparent, prompt and low-friction.
- Uncapped scheduled fee-for-service mainly expands low-value repetition rather than clinically useful access.
- Place-based accountability does not prevent cherry-picking or inequitable under-service.
- Scope-enabled supply fragments care or worsens safety and re-presentation.
- Current reforms already deliver the access, equity, rural, urgent-care and hospital-pressure gains this architecture is meant to add.

## Claim boundary

Uncapped does not mean uncontrolled; it means scheduled, rules-based, audited and place-accountable.

Public wording should stay at the right level:

- Public documents show...
- Theory predicts...
- Stakeholder feedback suggests...
- The benchmark shows the logic of...
- I think this warrants testing...
