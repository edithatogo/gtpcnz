# Research audit index — v0.4.1

## Purpose

This audit pack demonstrates the work artefacts underpinning the New Zealand game-theory mapping at the current conceptual stage. It is designed to make the mapping reviewable, contestable and ready for formal research development.

## Current audit position

The v0.4.1 mapping is **not an empirical finding**. It is a structured, theory-informed, source-linked policy hypothesis.

The artefacts currently support four propositions:

1. the broader policy problem can be decomposed into interacting games;
2. each game can be mapped to specific players, strategies, payoffs and expected failure modes;
3. each mapped mechanism can be linked to at least one of public evidence, economic theory, modelling assumption or stakeholder validation requirement;
4. the mapping can be converted into simulation scenarios, evidence-review questions, policy briefs and an NZMJ-style conceptual or modelling paper.

## What remains incomplete

The main remaining audit gaps are:

- no completed systematic or scoping review yet;
- no empirical stakeholder interviews yet;
- no OIA responses yet for unreleased PHO/funding documents;
- no calibrated simulation results yet;
- no formal equity impact analysis yet;
- no independent Māori, Pacific, rural or consumer review yet;
- no economic evaluation yet.

## Bottom line

At this stage, the audit pack is sufficient to show that the mapping is structured and traceable. It is not sufficient to claim that the proposed National Primary Care Benefits Schedule is empirically proven.
