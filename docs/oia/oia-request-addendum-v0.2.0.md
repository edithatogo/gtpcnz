# OIA request addendum: capitation formula, PHO advice and payment architecture

## Operational capitation formula and calculation workbook

Under the Official Information Act 1982, I request the following information relating to current and planned primary care capitation funding:

1. The current full operational capitation calculation formula used for general practice / PHO capitation payments.
2. All current rate tables, weightings, coefficients, cohort definitions and add-on formulae used to calculate payments.
3. Any calculation workbook, model code, data dictionary, algorithm, implementation guide or technical schedule used by the Ministry of Health, Health New Zealand, PHOs or payment services to calculate capitation payments.
4. All schedules or appendices of the current PHO Services Agreement that define, modify or operationalise capitation, VLCA, Community Services Card, Services to Improve Access, Care Plus, high-user, rural, deprivation, age, ethnicity, morbidity or other relevant payment streams.
5. Current pass-through rules or expectations governing how PHOs distribute first-contact capitation and related payments to practices.
6. Any implementation model, workbook or technical paper for the planned 1 July 2026 capitation reweighting.
7. Any advice comparing the current capitation formula with a direct-claim or benefits-schedule model for primary care contacts.
8. Any advice assessing whether capitation reweighting alone is expected to increase appointment supply, reduce closed books, reduce patient co-payments, or reduce hospital/ED demand.

If parts of the calculation model are withheld, please provide the remaining material and identify which grounds for withholding apply to each withheld component.

## PHO roles, functions and options for change

Under the Official Information Act 1982, I request:

1. Briefing H2025067082, referred to in H2025069314 as previous advice on PHOs' roles, functions and options for change.
2. Any attachments, appendices, aide-memoires, talking points, decision slides, Cabinet material, Ministerial annotations or related documents concerning H2025067082.
3. Any advice prepared since 1 January 2024 on whether PHOs should remain the gateway for public primary care funding.
4. Any advice prepared since 1 January 2024 on direct payment to primary care providers, direct claiming, national payment platforms, HICAPS-like infrastructure, or MBS-like primary care payment architecture.
5. Any analysis of PHO transaction costs, reserves, pass-through rates, administrative costs, ownership structures, monitoring constraints or market-entry effects.
6. Any advice on separating PHO population-health/locality functions from payment intermediation functions.

## Ambulance and primary care substitution

Under the Official Information Act 1982, I request advice, reports or modelling since 1 January 2023 assessing:

1. ambulance hear-and-treat, treat-and-refer, non-conveyance and alternative destination pathways;
2. the potential of these pathways to reduce ED demand or hospital admissions;
3. funding models for paramedic-generated care outside conveyance;
4. coordination between Health NZ and ACC funding for ambulance/prehospital care;
5. whether ambulance performance indicators should be included in top-tier national health targets.
