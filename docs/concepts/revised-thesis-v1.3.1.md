# Revised thesis — uncapped primary medical FFS stream — v1.3.1

## Core thesis

The problem is not only that capitation weights need updating, nor only that primary care needs a protected pool of money. The deeper problem is that a capped or effectively fixed primary care funding envelope can suppress marginal supply even when patient need exists.

The policy objective should therefore be to move part of primary care funding toward a **demand-led, ACC-like fee-for-service medical benefit stream**.

## Preferred architecture

> **Capitation + uncapped medical fee-for-service + place-based population accountability.**

This means:

1. **Capitation remains** for continuity, enrolled-population accountability, baseline viability, care coordination and proactive care.
2. **A new uncapped primary medical FFS stream** funds eligible medical primary care contacts as activity occurs, like an entitlement rather than a capped programme.
3. **The public contribution is scheduled**: the item price is fixed, but the total volume is not fixed by a hard annual primary-care envelope.
4. **Co-payment settings help calibrate demand**, but equity protections prevent price becoming the mechanism of unmet need.
5. **Clinical governance, scope-of-practice rules and audit** define who can do what safely.
6. **Place-based accountability** prevents cherry-picking and protects complex, rural, high-need and hard-to-reach populations.
7. **Urgent care, ambulance alternatives and ACC interactions** are treated as part of the same upstream access game.

## What this is not

It is not a return to unconstrained low-value activity. It is not an uncapped blank cheque. It is not a proposal to remove all controls.

It is a shift from:

> **global cap / constrained envelope / rationing by supply**

Towards:

> **open-ended entitlement for eligible contacts / scheduled prices / clinical rules / audit / co-payment protections / place accountability.**

## Public-facing line

> **Primary care funding should stop capping the total amount of eligible care people can receive. It should pay a scheduled contribution when eligible care is actually delivered, while using clinical rules, co-payment protections and place-based accountability to stop waste and protect equity.**

## Policy-facing line

> **The missing element in current reform is an uncapped, demand-led primary medical benefit stream that restores a marginal payment signal for clinically necessary primary care activity, analogous to ACC treatment contributions, while retaining capitation and adding place-based population obligations.**
