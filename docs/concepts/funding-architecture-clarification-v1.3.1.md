# Funding architecture clarification — caps, entitlement and FFS — v1.3.1

## The distinction that matters

There are three different things that can be called a “cap”. They should not be conflated.

| Type of cap | Keep or remove? | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| **Global budget cap / fixed annual primary care quantum** | Remove for eligible primary medical activity | It suppresses marginal supply and channels unmet need into urgent, ambulance and hospital systems. |
| **Scheduled item prices / public contribution rates** | Keep | Prices need to be predictable and fiscally managed. |
| **Clinical eligibility, scope, frequency, duration and audit controls** | Keep and strengthen | These prevent low-value activity, unsafe care and gaming. |

## Proposed funding compartments

| Compartment | Payment logic | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| **Capitation** | Fixed payment per enrolled person, weighted by need | Continuity, baseline viability, care coordination, proactive care and population accountability. |
| **Medical FFS stream** | Demand-led scheduled contribution for eligible primary medical contacts | Marginal supply, access, urgent care, procedures, complex contacts and timely assessment. |
| **Place-based commissioning** | Population/geographic accountability payments and contracts | Equity, outreach, rural resilience, integration and hard-to-reach groups. |
| **Programme/bundled payments** | Condition/pathway/team-based payments | Planned care, multidisciplinary care and longer-term management. |
| **Ambulance/urgent alternatives** | Treat-and-refer, hear-and-treat, urgent care and alternative pathway funding | Hospital deflection and safe upstream access. |

## The ACC analogy

The analogy is not that ordinary health need is an ACC claim. The analogy is that ACC demonstrates how New Zealand can operate a rules-based, activity-sensitive treatment contribution system. A primary care equivalent would need its own eligibility rules, item schedule, co-payment settings, equity protections and quality controls.

## Modelling implication

The preferred scenario should now be labelled:

> **Uncapped primary medical FFS entitlement + capitation + place-based accountability.**

A weaker scenario is:

> **Uncapped FFS without place accountability and weak controls.**

That weaker scenario is deliberately high risk because it could increase activity while worsening equity, cherry-picking, fiscal leakage and low-value billing.
