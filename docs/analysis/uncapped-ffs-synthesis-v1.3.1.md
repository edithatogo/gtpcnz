# v1.3.1 synthesis — uncapping primary care funding

## What changed from v1.3.0

The policy proposition has been clarified. The aim is not just a ring-fenced, protected or larger capped primary care budget. It is to remove the hard cap on eligible primary medical activity and introduce an ACC-style, fee-for-service public contribution stream for eligible primary medical contacts.

## Revised architecture

> **Capitation + uncapped primary medical FFS + place-based population accountability.**

## Why this matters

A capped primary care funding envelope can look fiscally controlled while pushing rationing into waiting times, closed books, co-payments, urgent care, ambulance and hospitals. A demand-led fee-for-service stream restores a marginal payment signal for additional clinically necessary care.

## Why safeguards still matter

Uncapped activity without safeguards can create low-value volume, cherry-picking and fiscal leakage. The preferred model therefore removes the global cap but keeps:

- scheduled item values;
- eligibility rules;
- clinical necessity and scope requirements;
- documentation and audit;
- co-payment protections;
- place-based obligations;
- data visibility through the National Primary Care Dataset;
- top-tier primary, urgent and ambulance KPIs.

## Updated conclusion

> **Do not cap the total amount of eligible primary medical care. Cap the item prices, define the rules, audit the claims, protect high-need patients, and hold the system accountable for whole populations.**
