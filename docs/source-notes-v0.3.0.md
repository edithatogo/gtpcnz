# Source notes v0.3.0

## NZ capitation and reweighting

The Ministry of Health describes capitation as the core way general practice is funded and states that the formula introduced in 2002 has not had major changes. Current reweighting is expected to include age, sex, multimorbidity/morbidity, rurality and deprivation. The released Sapere briefing states that the work was a technical analysis to improve the capitation formula and did not consider fundamental changes to the overall primary care funding model.

## National Primary Care Dataset and access target

Health NZ material says the first focus of the National Primary Care Dataset is general practice encounter and appointment information, linked to the target that 80% of primary care appointments are accessed within seven days of booking.

## PHO intermediation

The 2020 Health and Disability System Review recommended that Tier 1 funding be protected and that there should be no requirement to contract through the national PHO Services Agreement. A 2025 Ministry briefing on PHO finances says the Ministry does not have direct access to PHO financial activity and ownership structures, limiting monitoring and regulation.

## Ambulance and prehospital care

Health NZ states that it commissions emergency ambulance services on behalf of Health NZ and ACC. Medical patients are funded by Health NZ, injured patients by ACC, and ambulance providers report performance data including response-time KPIs, demand, quality and call type.

## Australia

The Australian Review of General Practice Incentives was delivered in 2024 and proposed a new funding architecture for primary care practices. The wider Australian system remains jointly run by federal, state and territory, and local governments, creating structurally different incentives from New Zealand.

## NZMJ

NZMJ author guidance requires Articles to have a structured abstract using Aims, Methods, Results and Conclusions; the author instructions also state that Original Articles and Viewpoints should use a structured abstract and that Original Articles have a 3000-word target.
