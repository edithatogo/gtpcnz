# Why hospitals win the funding game

Hospitals do not win the funding game because hospital leaders are villains. They win because the rules make hospital pressure visible, urgent and politically unavoidable.

A person who cannot get primary care may delay. The delay may be invisible. The practice may absorb the stress. The patient may pay more. The problem may sit at home.

Then one day the same unmet need appears in an emergency department. Now it is visible. It has a wait time, a target, a bed, a queue, a news story, a ministerial briefing and a budget pressure.

By then, the system must respond.

That is the repeated game. Underfund the earlier setting, then rescue the later setting. Do it enough times and the hospital becomes the growth sector by default.

The solution is not to take funding from hospitals. Hospitals are necessary and many are already under pressure. The solution is to stop designing the upstream system so tightly that hospitals become the unavoidable release valve.

Primary care and ambulance services should not simply be inputs into hospital performance. They should be top-tier performance domains of the health system in their own right.

Access to primary care, urgent community care, ambulance response, safe non-conveyance, treat-and-refer care, co-payment burden, closed books and ambulatory-sensitive hospitalisations should be reported with the same seriousness as ED and elective targets.

If the system only measures the hospital crisis, it will keep funding the hospital crisis.
