# MCDA update — uncapped FFS stream — v1.3.1

The MCDA now needs to distinguish **uncapped eligible activity** from **uncapped weak-control activity**.

## New criterion

### Marginal supply restoration / removal of global cap

Question: Does the option remove a hard global funding constraint on eligible primary medical contacts and create a meaningful payment signal for additional clinically necessary care?

Score:

- **0**: no marginal payment signal; fixed envelope remains dominant.
- **1**: modest marginal payment for selected activities only.
- **2**: scheduled benefits for defined contacts, but limited eligibility or volume controls still suppress supply.
- **3**: demand-led scheduled benefits for eligible care with item-level controls rather than a global cap.

## New risk penalty

### Uncapped weak-control risk

Penalise options that remove the global cap but lack:

- item definitions;
- clinical necessity;
- provider scope and credentialing;
- frequency/duration rules;
- co-payment protections;
- place-based accountability;
- audit and low-value activity controls.

## Revised preferred option label

Replace:

> National Primary Care Benefits Schedule + place-based population accountability

with:

> **Uncapped primary medical FFS entitlement + capitation + place-based population accountability.**
