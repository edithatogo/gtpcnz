# Stakeholder workshop guide v0.9.0

## Purpose

To test the game map and MCDA with stakeholders before moving to empirical calibration or policy endorsement.

## Suggested participants

- General practice owners and salaried clinicians.
- Nurse practitioners, pharmacists, allied health providers and paramedics.
- PHO/locality leaders.
- Maori and Pacific providers.
- Rural providers.
- ACC, Health NZ, Ministry and Treasury officials or former officials.
- Ambulance and emergency care leaders.
- Hospital executives and ED clinicians.
- Consumer and patient advocates.

## Session structure

### Part 1: Orient to the thesis

Present the thesis:

> Tightly managing primary care and ambulance activity may channel growth into hospitals by default.

Clarify that the work is a falsifiable policy hypothesis, not proof.

### Part 2: Game-position scoring

Participants score each game using the game-position template. Capture individual and group ratings.

### Part 3: Policy-option scoring

Participants score the policy options using the policy-option MCDA template.

### Part 4: Weighting exercise

Participants choose or construct a weight set. Suggested starting sets are balanced, equity-focused, fiscal-control, rural-access, hospital-pressure, and market-entry focused.

### Part 5: Sensitivity and disagreement

Review how rankings change by weight set. Ask where disagreement reflects values, evidence gaps, implementation risk, professional interests or fiscal assumptions.

### Part 6: Validation backlog

Turn uncertainty into validation tasks: data requests, OIA requests, modelling tests and stakeholder interviews.

## Outputs

- Group-weighted MCDA results.
- Stakeholder-specific rankings.
- Disagreement map.
- Validation backlog.
- Revised game map.
