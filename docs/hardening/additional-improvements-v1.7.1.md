# Additional improvements considered in v1.7.1

The main recommendation is to harden and simplify, not expand. Additional improvements considered and incorporated:

1. **Evidence gates:** public posts may proceed, but implementation claims require stakeholder validation, OIA responses and empirical data.
2. **Kill criteria:** a `what would change my mind` table now defines what evidence would weaken the thesis.
3. **Parameter tiering:** the 70-parameter scaffold is tiered to avoid over-claiming.
4. **Identifiability mapping:** each parameter is labelled by how it could be observed or estimated.
5. **RACMA exclusion:** RACMA is no longer an active pathway; they may re-enter if interested.
6. **Front-door simplification:** launch with the landing page, Post 1, visual summary, objections brief and model card.
7. **First-six-post gate:** only polish the first six before launch; revise the back half after feedback.
8. **Figure-caption cautions:** plots must identify whether they are conceptual, model-generated or empirical.
9. **Source-confidence labels:** separate public documents, theory, stakeholder intelligence, model assumptions and policy judgement.
10. **Red-team action register:** known vulnerabilities are tracked explicitly.

No additional modelling is recommended until stakeholder validation or OIA/data responses change the evidence base.
