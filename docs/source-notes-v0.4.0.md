# Source notes v0.4.0

## New Zealand primary care and capitation

1. Ministry of Health / Health NZ. The Sapere report and re-weighting primary care capitation funding. Briefing H2024057558, 12 December 2024. Key points: capitation introduced in 2002; formula based on use patterns; not fundamentally changed; age and sex variables limited the model; Sapere work technical and did not consider fundamental changes to the overall funding model.

2. Health NZ. National Primary Care Dataset and new primary care health target. Key points: dataset will collect general practice encounter and appointment information; first focus supports target for 80% access within seven days; general practice encounter and booking appointment data flows begin April 2026; target reporting includes encounter and appointment data from July 2026.

3. Health NZ. Primary Care Tactical Action Plan and primary care health data material. Key points: new primary care target and outcomes framework; performance-related funding and dataset development.

## Health system roles and PHOs

4. Ministry of Health. Health Crown entities. Key point: Health NZ is responsible for planning and commissioning hospital, primary and community health services.

5. Health and Disability System Review. Final report and section C. Key points: Tier 1 funding/contracting should be more flexible; funding should protect and expand community-based care; PHO contracting should not be mandatory for all primary care services.

6. Ministry of Health. Briefing H2025063254, Background on Primary Health Organisations. Key point: the 2020 Health and Disability System Review recommended DHBs no longer be required to contract through PHOs.

7. Ministry of Health. Briefing H2025069314, PHO finances: a summary of available information. Key point: the Ministry does not have direct access to PHO financial activity and ownership structures, constraining monitoring and regulation.

## Ambulance

8. Health NZ. The Ambulance Team. Key points: the Ambulance Team commissions emergency ambulance services on behalf of Health NZ and ACC; medical patients are funded by Health NZ and injured patients by ACC; ambulance providers report response-time KPIs, demand, quality of service and call-type information.

## Australia comparator

9. Australian Government Department of Health, Disability and Ageing. The Australian health system. Key point: the system is jointly run by Australian, state and territory governments; Medicare subsidises services through the MBS.

10. Australian Government Department of Health and Aged Care. Review of General Practice Incentives: Expert Advisory Panel report, 2024. Key point: supports simplified blended payment architecture and broader multidisciplinary primary care teams.
