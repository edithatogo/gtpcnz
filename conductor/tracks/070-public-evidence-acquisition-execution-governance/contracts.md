# Contracts

- Public evidence acquisition execution may use public or published aggregate sources only.
- Execution records must distinguish source discovery, candidate review, raw retrieval, checksum verification, transform, schema validation, and calibration/validation gate review.
- Missing raw files, missing checksums, unclear licence/access status, missing processed artefacts, or failed schema checks must produce explicit blockers rather than placeholder data.
- CAL-G-002 may pass only through registered public temporal comparisons. CAL-G-003, CAL-G-004, and CAL-G-005 remain readiness-only or failed until registered public comparisons pass their gates.
- Governance records may authorise an acquisition attempt, but they must not claim precise fiscal savings, ED reductions, hospital-demand reductions, workforce effects, implementation impacts, or causal effects.
- No private administrative data, patient-level data, confidential OIA responses, stakeholder analysis, or unpublished expert elicitation may enter the public runtime path.
- Diagnostics may explain tolerance gaps and evidence requirements, but must not tune thresholds to force a pass.
