# Track 009 — Research audit and traceability pack

## Purpose

Create a defensible audit trail for the New Zealand primary care funding game map, showing how the work artefacts were produced, what sources support the mapping, what assumptions remain untested, and what further empirical work is required.

## Scope

This track covers the audit of the conceptual and modelling artefacts at v0.4.1. It does not claim empirical validation of the hypothesis.

## Deliverables

- Audit index and artefact manifest.
- Claim-evidence-assumption register.
- Game-map traceability matrix.
- Decision log.
- Source-quality and search log.
- Reproducibility checklist aligned to PRISMA-ScR, STRESS, ODD and CHEERS where relevant.
- Falsification and sensitivity register.
- Stakeholder validation plan.

## Completion criteria

The mapping work should be reviewable by an external colleague who can see:

1. the claim being made;
2. the game layer in which it appears;
3. the evidence or theory supporting it;
4. the artefact in which it is used;
5. the assumption that remains to be tested;
6. the proposed method for testing or falsifying it.
