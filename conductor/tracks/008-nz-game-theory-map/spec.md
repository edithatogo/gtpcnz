# Track 008 spec: NZ broader policy game-theory map

## Purpose

Make the game-theoretic analysis of New Zealand primary care, ambulance, PHO, ACC, workforce-scope and hospital budget architecture explicit enough to support policy briefs, Substack posts, modelling and an NZMJ article.

## Scope

This track maps the broader NZ policy problem as a nested set of games rather than one simple normal-form game. It covers:

- Health NZ hospital-operating and primary-care-commissioning tensions.
- Capitation reweighting versus contact-type benefits.
- PHO intermediation and transaction costs.
- Provider supply, scope-of-practice and professional bottlenecks.
- Consumer co-payment and congestion choices.
- Ambulance conveyance versus hear-and-treat/treat-and-refer incentives.
- ACC injury-care spillovers into general practice viability.
- Telehealth adverse selection and rural local-supply erosion.
- KPI/reporting games.
- Equity and Te Tiriti design constraints.

## Non-goals

- Do not present the model as empirical proof.
- Do not assert PHO abolition as a settled conclusion.
- Do not claim capitation is inherently wrong; the focus is the failure mode of dominant capitation under supply constraint.
- Do not remove clinical safety or professional scope constraints; remove funding-model constraints that artificially restrict eligible supply.
